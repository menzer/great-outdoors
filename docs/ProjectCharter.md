# Project Charter

Project Name: **Pacific Northwest Hiking Web App**

Project Sponsor: **Poul Nichols**



## 1. Project Purpose / Background
*(Why this project is being undertaken; business need or problem it addresses)*

- New hikers struggle to find accurate, centralized, and up-to-date trail information
- Popular trails are overcrowded, while lesser-known trails are underused
- Many websites are outdated, difficult to navigate, or focused mainly on advertising gear rather than safety and stewardship



## 2. Project Objectives
*(What the project aims to achieve; should be specific and measurable if possible)*

- Provide reliable trail information (difficulty, distance, elevation gain)
- Promote safe hiking practices and seasonal awareness
- Encourage responsible outdoor recreation and environmental stewardship
- Highlight lesser-known trails to reduce overcrowding
- Build a community around hiking in the PNW

### Success Criteria

- Meeting the desired amount of monthly active users
- Raising a targeted amount of money through the app
- Meeting a targeted amount of engagement through trail reviews, ratings, and hike registrations



## 3. Scope
### In Scope:

- Trail review system
- Donation/payment portal
- List of local hiking groups/clubs
- Easily readable and accessible information about hiking trails in PNW
- Links to other education materials about safe hiking practices

### Out of Scope:

- GPS tracking
- Actual technical integration with partner applications
- Merchandise/e-commerce store for nonprofits and fundraising



## 4. High-Level Deliverables
*(Key outputs/products of the project)*

- Deliverable 1: Design deliverables for review and approval
- Deliverable 2: Website that meets the required functionality
