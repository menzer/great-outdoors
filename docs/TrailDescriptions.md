# Green Lake Loop
#### ℹ️ Easy, Wheelchairs, Parking, Bikes, Kayaks, Swimming
*2.8mi, 0ft*

Just north of Seattle's Downtown is Green Lake. In such an urban
area, Green Lake has been shaped into part of the urban landscape,
providing all sorts of recreation activities accessible to everyone.
The loop consists of a paved path around the edge of the lake, and
provides lanes for both pedestrians and cyclists. Along the loop,
there are many opportunities to explore parks, get food and coffee,
go swimming, visit the zoo, play sports, and more! Green Lake is a
go-to destination for summer time fun.



# Washington Park Arboretum Loop
#### ℹ️ Easy, Wheelchairs, Parking, Picnic, Flowers, Forest
*2.3mi, 147ft*

Just South of the University of Washington campus is the
Washington Park Arboretum. This park offers a wide variety of plants, 
along with information currated by UW. The walk is mostly covered in
foleage, save for a spur of the trail that takes you to see views
across Lake Washington. In the spring, the park is bathed in a wide
variety of colors from all of the blooming plants. 



# Discovery Park Loop
#### ℹ️ Easy, Parking, Bikes, Beach, Lighthouse, Viewpoints
*4.4mi | 400ft*

On the tip of the Magnolia peninsula is Discovery Park. With forest, meadows, and coasts, Discovery Park offers a taste of the best of the
great outdoors without leaving the comfort of Seattle. The
expedition to the tip of the peninsula to see the lighthouse
is worth it to see the sweeping views across the Puget Sound.
The Discovery Park Loop trail is an excelent introduction to
what hiking has to offer.
