5040 Peak

5.2mi | 3,260ft

Across the border and across the water, 5040 Peak lays near the center of Vancouver Island. Getting it's name from the elevation of the peak, it's about 5 miles of grueling uphill. There's not much to see for the first mile, but once you break through the tree line you get a stunning view of Triple Peak. Cobolt Lake marks the half way point. There are ample campsights and a composting outhouse. Camp sites are more competitive further up near the lodge, but the views are worth it. The final climb towards the top reveals rolling peaks with views to both the east and west coasts of the island.

Getting There

📍 49° 11' 30.5" N, 125° 16' 57.6" W

Traveling on BC-4, turn onto Marion Creek Forest Service Road. Continue to the trailhead. Parking is available at the trailhead.
